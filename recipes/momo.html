Momo

Description

Before we begin getting into the details of the recipe, let's talk about the origin of the dish. "Momo" is the Nepali name for dumplings and it originates from Tibet/Northern Nepal. There are different types of momo fillings that go inside the momo wraps depending on individual taste and dietary preferences. Although, there are different varieties of momo and they are cooked differently, all momos are initially steam cooked.

Momo is served with a spicy sauce made up of tomatoes, corriander, peppers, salt, MSG, and seasame seeds.

Ingredients

  • Veggies or Meats
  • Dumpling Wraps
  • MSG
  • Salt
  • Corriander Powder
  • Cumin Powder
  • Thai Chillies
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Ginger
  • Oil
  • Tomatoes
  • Seasame Seeds
  • Water

Steps

  1. Grind the veggies or meat using a large grinder and season it with MSG, salt, corriander Powder, cumin powder one garlic, two onions and four tablespoon of oil. (Use MSG, salt and spices as per your taste preferences) This will be the filling that goes inside the dumpling.
  2. Scoop a spoonfull of seasoned filling and place it inside the dumpling wraps one at time.
  3. Carefully close the opening of the wrap. To ensure it doesn't break, use water to stick the opening together.
  4. Once desired quantity of dumplings are wrapped, oil your steamer and place the dumplings on it to be steamed.
  5. To make the sauce, take one or two tomatoes and boil it for three minutes.
  6. Once tomatoes are boiled, put it in a grinder along with corriander leafs, salt, MSG, half a lemon, half red onion, one thai chilly garlic, and seasame seeds and grind it into a thick paste. The sauce is ready.
  7. After 20 minutes of steaming, the momo is ready to be taken out of the steamer and eaten.
  8. Enjoy the tasty dumplings with the homemade sauce.
